McConnell Dowell - Project 8303 Edgecumbe WWTP - Doses calibrated to Beca Process Design Report (8303-BEC-RPT-PA-0001) + IXOM quote (May 2026). Sludge basis: iSBR 4,487 kg/d (Beca Process Design Report Table 6) = 2,752 biological + 1,735 alum; MBR biological component at parity with iSBR (Wei et al. 2003; Qiblawey & Judd 2019) + 20% higher alum sludge (single-point dosing on MBR). iSBR energy 19,000 kWh/d (Beca Load List rev 2, 18 May 2026); MBR energy 23,200 kWh/d (iSBR base + Beca Op_Costs membrane-specific + high-MLSS aeration premium + scour correction). Energy intensity consistent with dairy WWTP data in Żyłka et al. (2021). Membrane life 5 yr (Zhang et al. 2021; IDF Bulletin 500/2019 dairy range 2-5 yr). Creep resets at membrane replacement. Expected Case CIP: 1 MC/week, RC every 60 days, 3 active trains, 2.0 kg Cl2/MC/cell. Maintenance: iSBR $350k, MBR $550k (project allowance benchmarked to comparable Australian MBR operating sites). Membrane disposal $30k/replacement event added. IDF + Conservative scenarios subject to internal review. HNO3 + Mg(OH)2 doses still placeholder pending Fonterra alkalinity data.
Electricity costed on energy basis (c/kWh) only. Industrial tariffs typically include demand and capacity components; for a sub-load on Fonterra's existing high-load site connection these are not expected to materially differentiate iSBR from MBR (both options run continuous baseload, marginal difference ~83 kW).
